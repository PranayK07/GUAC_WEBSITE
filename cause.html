Why are we making this filter?





The rise of carbon dioxide emissions negatively affecting urban and suburban communities
These rising emissions are caused by a larger population requiring more resources, products, and transportation to survive, slowly causing more emissions
Emissions have risen by 90% since 1970, and are projected to keep rising
Countries such as China, the United States, Russia, Germany, the United Kingdom, Japan, and India, all of which have growing economies and populations, are the main concern when dealing with issues of current and future emmissions

Undeniably, the atmosphere is devastatingly changing. A considerable amount of this change is a result of the human emissions of carbon from multiple industrial inventions. Ever since the industrial age, many humans have been striving to make daily tasks more efficient. Does it take too long to travel from Point A to Point B? That's fine, invent a car. Do you ever wish you had a nice source of fuel? That's fine, check out fossil fuels! These types of inventions have led to a lot of carbon emissions into the atmosphere which has caused the atmosphere to change greatly. Never has there ever been this amount of change in the atmosphere ever since we have touched this planet. This change has also made it hard for humans and other animals survive, it has also changed the temperature of our planet. They have also melted a lot of the ice we find at the North and South poles.
